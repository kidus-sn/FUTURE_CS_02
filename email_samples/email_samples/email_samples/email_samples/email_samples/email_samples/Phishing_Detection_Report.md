# 1. Introduction

Phishing is one of the most common cyber threats used to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, financial data, and personal information. This project analyzes email samples and classifies them as Safe, Suspicious, or Phishing based on common phishing indicators.

# 2. Methodology

Each email was analyzed using the following criteria:

- Sender legitimacy
- Domain authenticity
- Presence of suspicious links
- Urgency or fear tactics
- Requests for sensitive information
- Grammar and writing quality

# 4. Common Phishing Indicators Identified

## Fake Sender Domains

Attackers often imitate trusted companies using unofficial domains.

## Urgency and Fear Tactics

Messages frequently pressure users to act immediately.

## Suspicious Links

Links may redirect users to fake login pages designed to steal credentials.

## Credential Harvesting

Phishing emails often attempt to collect usernames and passwords.

# 5. User Awareness Recommendations

1. Verify sender email addresses carefully.
2. Avoid clicking unknown links.
3. Never provide passwords through email.
4. Enable multi-factor authentication.
5. Confirm suspicious requests through official channels.
6. Report phishing attempts to security teams.

# 6. Conclusion

This analysis demonstrated how phishing attacks commonly use fraudulent sender addresses, urgency, and deceptive links to manipulate users. By recognizing these indicators and following security best practices, users can significantly reduce their risk of becoming victims of phishing attacks.
